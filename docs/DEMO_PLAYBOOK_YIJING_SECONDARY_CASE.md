# Yijing Secondary Case Playbook

Date: 2026-05-16

## Goal

Use the Yijing case as a secondary demo after the Smart Notes product-method demo. Its job is to prove that Yansilu can handle complex semantic relationships, not to define the product as a Yijing knowledge base.

## Setup

- Open `/demo/yijing` for the case story page.
- Open `/prototype?demo=yijing-rich` for the interactive workspace.
- Start from the graph view, then show index cards and writing projects.

## Talk Track

### 0:00-0:25 - Position The Case

Say clearly: this is the second case. The primary demo is `/demo/zettelkasten`, where the product workflow is easier to understand end to end.

The Yijing case exists because it stresses the graph: many notes, many relation types, and multiple writing projects.

### 0:25-1:10 - Show Complex Relations

Open the graph and point to relation types such as supports, qualifies, contrasts, counterexample, and appears in draft.

Emphasize that a relationship is only useful when the rationale can be inspected. A beautiful graph without reasons is not enough for serious thinking.

### 1:10-1:45 - Show Product Discipline

Explain that the product treats the topic as structured thinking:

- permanent notes carry claims
- relations carry reasons
- index cards organize entry points
- writing projects turn the graph into output

Avoid spending time explaining the Yijing content itself. The demo is about the product behavior around complex material.

### 1:45-2:20 - Show Writing Readiness

Open the writing project or scaffold and show how graph material enters a draft plan.

Tie it back to the same product promise as Smart Notes: material should become traceable writing preparation, not a static archive.

## Guardrails

- Do not use Yijing as the homepage headline or primary product identity.
- Do not present the case as a finished knowledge encyclopedia.
- Always return to the product concept: relation reasons, traceable judgment, and writing readiness.
- Keep the Smart Notes demo as the recommended first demo.

## Acceptance Checklist

- `/demo/yijing` is clearly framed as the second case.
- The CTA still opens `/prototype?demo=yijing-rich`.
- The speaker explains relation reasons as the key product proof.
- The case complements, rather than replaces, the Smart Notes workflow demo.
