# Marketing Site Homepage V1

## Goal

Create a homepage for Yansilu that explains the product's philosophy clearly, differentiates it from generic AI note tools, and drives users toward registration, login, and paid conversion.

Core positioning:

`Yansilu is not built to help people save more notes. It is built to help them turn knowledge into their own wisdom.`

## Target Audience

Primary audiences:

- Students and researchers
- Knowledge writers
- Independent creators who build long-form ideas

Common pain points:

- They collect a lot, but do not form stable understanding
- They write many notes, but do not produce reusable ideas
- They restart from scratch when writing
- They want AI help, but do not want to outsource their thinking

## Homepage Job

The homepage should do four things well:

1. Explain what the product is in one glance
2. Make the philosophy memorable
3. Show the workflow from source material to writing
4. Lead users into `Register`, `Login`, or `Upgrade`

## Narrative Spine

Homepage story:

1. You already have too much material
2. The real problem is not collection, but digestion
3. Yansilu helps you compress material into clear viewpoints
4. Those viewpoints become connected notes and writing structure
5. AI assists the process, but does not replace authorship

## Page Structure

### 1. Hero

Headline:

`Turn knowledge into your own wisdom.`

Subheadline:

`A thinking workspace for research, writing, and long-term knowledge building. Not an AI that writes for you, but a system that helps ideas take shape.`

Primary CTA:

`Start Free`

Secondary CTA:

`See the Philosophy`

Supporting visual direction:

- A calm, editorial product composition
- Notes, links, and distilled statements moving from scattered inputs to a clear central thesis
- Dark green brand foundation from the existing icon, with warm paper-like light surfaces for contrast

### 2. Problem Section

Section title:

`You do not need a bigger inbox. You need clearer thought.`

Supporting copy:

`Most tools help you save more. Few help you understand more.`

Four problem statements:

- `You collect articles, highlights, and fragments, but they stay external to your thinking.`
- `You write notes, but they do not become reusable ideas.`
- `When it is time to write, you search again instead of building forward.`
- `AI can generate text quickly, but speed does not guarantee originality.`

### 3. Philosophy Section

Section title:

`A note system designed to turn knowledge into wisdom.`

Three principles:

- `Understanding before storage`
- `Compression before composition`
- `Assistance without surrendering authorship`

Explanatory copy:

`Yansilu treats a note not as a container, but as a unit of thinking. It asks for paraphrase, thesis, and structure, so ideas become usable later.`

### 4. Workflow Section

Section title:

`From material to manuscript.`

Workflow steps:

1. `Capture source material`
2. `Write literature notes`
3. `Distill permanent notes`
4. `Organize with index cards`
5. `Build a writing scaffold`

Section goal:

Show that the product is a chain, not a pile of features.

### 5. Signature Features Section

Section title:

`What makes it different`

Feature cards:

- `Paraphrase-first note taking`
  - `Do not stop at excerpts. Turn reading into your own language.`
- `One-sentence thesis`
  - `Each permanent note should carry a clear judgment, not a vague topic.`
- `Three-line compression`
  - `Reduce a thought until it becomes reusable in writing and discussion.`
- `Index cards for themes`
  - `Indexes are not tags. They are structured thinking surfaces.`
- `Traceable writing scaffold`
  - `Every section can point back to notes and sources.`
- `AI with boundaries`
  - `AI suggests, checks, and clarifies. It does not secretly write the final thought for you.`

### 6. Audience Section

Section title:

`Built for people who think by writing`

Audience blocks:

- `Researchers`
  - `Turn reading notes into arguments and literature structure.`
- `Writers`
  - `Accumulate durable ideas instead of isolated drafts.`
- `Knowledge creators`
  - `Build topic libraries, recurring themes, and long-form output systems.`

### 7. Trust and Product Boundaries Section

Section title:

`Your ideas stay yours`

Trust points:

- `Local-first content model`
- `Markdown as the core format`
- `Exportable notes and structures`
- `AI outputs should remain traceable and reviewable`

This section matters because the product position is closely tied to ownership and authorship.

### 8. Pricing Preview Section

Section title:

`Start with the workflow. Upgrade for deeper assistance.`

Pricing shape:

- `Free`
  - `Core note workflow, local usage, foundational organization`
- `Pro`
  - `Knowledge-to-wisdom assistance, AI suggestions, structured writing support, enhanced search and import`

Primary CTA:

`Start Free`

Secondary CTA:

`View Pricing`

### 9. Final CTA Section

Headline:

`Build a thinking practice, not just a note archive.`

Buttons:

- `Create Account`
- `Log In`

## Tone and Style

The homepage should feel:

- Reflective, not hyped
- Precise, not generic
- Editorial, not startup-template driven
- Warm and serious, not cold and technical

Words to emphasize:

- thinking
- distillation
- viewpoint
- structure
- authorship
- writing

Words to avoid overusing:

- productivity
- second brain
- smart notes
- AI copilot
- knowledge management

## Conversion Paths

Top-level user paths:

1. First-time visitor -> `Start Free` -> register
2. Returning user -> `Log In`
3. Curious evaluator -> philosophy and workflow -> pricing -> register
4. High-intent user -> homepage -> pricing -> checkout

## Follow-up Pages Needed

After the homepage, the next pages should be:

1. Pricing
2. Register
3. Login
4. Checkout
5. Billing / account
